Wiring Guide: TN2106 & TP2104

Pin Configuration

Pin Alignment Reference
  • Pin 1 (Left): Source (S)
  • Pin 2 (Center): Gate (G)
  • Pin 3 (Right): Drain (D)

Controlling Current Flow

Because of their complementary chemical behaviors, N-channel and P-channel MOSFETs are integrated into distinct areas of an electrical circuit loop to efficiently control the path of current.

TN2106
  • Source: Power goes out.
  • Drain: Power goes in.
  • Gate Control: Likes power. Applying a higher voltage attracts free electrons. This creates a conductive channel that links the Source and Drain pins together.
TP2104
  • Source: Power goes in.
  • Drain: Power goes out.
  • Gate Control: Likes ground. Applying a lower voltage attracts positive 'holes'. This creates a conductive channel that links the Source and Drain pins together.

Gate State Management

The insulated gate acts like a tiny, high-resistance storage tank for static electricity. If left disconnected ("floating"), random electrostatic charge or EM noise will accumulate on the gate, putting the transistor in an unpredictable state.

  • For TN2106 (N-Channel): Tie a 10kΩ Pull-Down Resistor between the Gate and Ground. This firmly establishes an "OFF" state whenever your control signal pin is in a high-impedance state or resetting.
  • For TP2104 (P-Channel): Tie a 10kΩ Pull-Up Resistor between the Gate and the Source rail. This keeps the VGS potential at 0V (fully "OFF") until actively driven low.

This is optional for Arduino Leonardo because for some reason, our circuits haven't burned down when we don't do this.
